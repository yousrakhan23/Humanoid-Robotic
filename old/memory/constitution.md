# Research paper on AI-native software development Constitution

## Core Principles

### I. Accuracy
All factual claims MUST be verified through primary source investigation.

### II. Clarity
Content MUST be clear and accessible to an academic audience with a computer science background.

### III. Reproducibility
All claims MUST be cited and traceable to their original sources, enabling independent verification.

### IV. Rigor
Preference MUST be given to peer-reviewed sources to ensure academic rigor and credibility.

## Key Standards
All factual claims MUST be traceable to specific sources.
Citation format MUST adhere to APA style guidelines.
Source types MUST include a minimum of 50% peer-reviewed articles.
Plagiarism MUST be 0% as detected by standard academic plagiarism tools before submission.
Writing clarity MUST target a Flesch-Kincaid grade level of 10-12 to ensure appropriate academic readability.

## Project Constraints & Success Criteria
**Constraints**:
Word count MUST be between 5,000-7,000 words.
A minimum of 15 distinct sources MUST be utilized.
The final paper MUST be delivered in PDF format with all citations properly embedded.

**Success Criteria**:
All claims MUST be verifiable against their cited sources.
Zero plagiarism MUST be detected by any review process.
The paper MUST pass all internal and external fact-checking reviews.

## Governance
Amendments to this constitution MUST be documented, approved by all stakeholders, and include a clear migration plan. All Pull Requests and reviews MUST verify compliance with these principles. Complexity in any aspect MUST be justified.
